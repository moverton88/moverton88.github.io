Selected Work

Improved Genotype Inference Reveals Cis- and Trans-Driven Variation in the Loss-of-Heterozygosity Rates in Yeast

Overton, M.S., Kryazhimskiy, S.

We develop a novel, reference genome-symmetric genotyping approach and simple corrections to address known issues in loss-of-heterozygosity (LOH) event detection and rate measurements. Our revised estimates derived from existing yeast hybrid datasets vary significanly from previously published ones and undermine certain existing inferences. Further, we construct a GLMM model to measure the contributions of genome-wide (trans) and local (cis) genetic factors. We find that the 60-fold variation in interstital LOH event rates across hybrids is driven overwhelmingly by trans effects, while the 6-fold variation in terminal LOH events is extremely noisy, with small contributions from both trans and cis factors.

Upper bound on the mutational burden imposed by a CRISPR-Cas9 Gene-Drive Element

Overton, M.S., Guy, S.E, ... Kryazhimskiy, S.

We test whether the presence of a CRISPR-Cas9 gene drive (CCGD) affects mutation accumulation in the yeast genome over evolutionary time-scales. The potential for these undesired effects raise potential concerns about the long-term evolutionary safety of CCGDs, but the magnitude of these effects is unknown. We unexpectedly found no detectable effects on the genome-wide rates of mutations or LOH events. Our power calculations suggest that CCGD elements affect these rates by less than 30%, which is much less than natural variation for these traits in yeast. A more detailed examination reveals only weak statistical support for the effect of CCGD elements on the length and genomic distributions of LOH events. Thus, our results demonstrate that CCGDs impose at most a weak additional mutational burden in the yeast model, and add credence to the proposition that the evolutionary risks posed by well-designed gene drives may be acceptable.
