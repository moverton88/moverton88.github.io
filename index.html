About Me

I am a scientist and educator dedicated to making scientific ideas accessible, engaging, and relevant to learners from all backgrounds. My teaching emphasizes academic empowerment, conceptual and social connection-building, and the holistic development of both students and communities. My research integrates experimental and computational approaches to explore genomic variation, gene regulation, and microbial physiology.

Teaching

Over nine years of experience teaching and mentoring in biology, including leading lab sections, designing curriculum, and fostering inclusive learning environments. I have taught courses in molecular biology, population genetics, microbiology, and bioinformatics at both UC San Diego and Cal Poly Pomona.

Research

My PhD research focused on the genetic and physiological basis of mutation-driven processes in yeast using whole-genome sequencing, CRISPR-based engineering, and custom bioinformatics pipelines. I also published a review on archaeal viruses during my master's program and am passionate about connecting data science with molecular biology.
